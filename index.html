What is a Genre?

When you think of genres, you may think of music genres, book genres, or movie genres, but those are only one subsection of genres. Genres “can be anything [that] responds to a [recurring] situation and has characteristics that make it recognizable” (47). For example, there are genres of pencils. There are colored pencils, mechanical pencils, watercolor pencils, and many more kinds; all of these are a genre of pencil.

So, take a second to think about something that responds to a recurring situation and has defined characteristics. Then think about the subsections and different kinds of that thing. Boom! Genres.

Here are more examples of what genres can look like!

Cake:

  • Red velvet
  • Chocolate
  • Vanilla

There is also boxed cake mix, premade cakes, and homemade cakes; all are a genre of cake.

Phones:

  • iPhones
  • Samsung
  • Flip phones
  • Nokia

Sororities:

  • Delta Delta Delta
  • Pi Beta Phi
  • Alpha Chi Omega
